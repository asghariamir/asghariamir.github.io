Challenge 1: Compute by Hand

With s = 2 and x₀ = 0.1, compute the first 5 iterates by hand. At each step, decide which branch applies (is xₙ ≤ ½ or > ½?), then apply the formula. Check your answers on the Explorer.

x₀ = 0.1 (left branch): x₁ = 2(0.1) = 0.2. x₁ = 0.2 (left): x₂ = 0.4. x₂ = 0.4 (left): x₃ = 0.8. x₃ = 0.8 (right): x₄ = 2(1−0.8) = 0.4. x₄ = 0.4 (left): x₅ = 0.8. The orbit settles into the pattern 0.4 → 0.8 → 0.4 → ... — a 2-cycle!

Challenge 3: Sensitive Dependence

Compare orbits from x₀ = 0.1 and x₀ = 0.101. Enable the second orbit on the Explorer. After how many steps do they diverge?

Since |T₂'| = 2 everywhere, the distance between two orbits doubles every step: |xₙ − yₙ| ≈ 2ⁿ|x₀ − y₀|. Starting 0.001 apart, after about 10 steps the separation is 0.001 × 2¹⁰ ≈ 1 — they're completely different! This is sensitive dependence on initial conditions: the hallmark of chaos.
