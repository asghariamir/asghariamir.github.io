üìê Essential Theory

From Newton to Secant

Newton's method replaces the function near $x_n$ by its tangent line, which requires evaluation of $f'(x_n)$. If the derivative is unavailable or expensive, we can approximate it using a finite difference based on two previous points.

Secant Approximation

Given two recent iterates $x_{n-1}$ and $x_n$, approximate the derivative by

$$f'(x_n) \approx \frac{f(x_n) - f(x_{n-1})}{x_n - x_{n-1}}$$

Substituting this into Newton's update gives the secant method:

$$x_{n+1} = x_n - f(x_n) \cdot \frac{x_n - x_{n-1}}{f(x_n) - f(x_{n-1})}$$
Key Idea

The secant method trades derivative information for memory: it uses past iterates to gain speed.

Convergence Behaviour

The secant method converges faster than linearly, but slower than Newton's method. Its order of convergence is approximately

$$p = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \approx 1.618$$

(the golden ratio!)

Order of Convergence (Informal)

If the error satisfies

$$|e_{n+1}| \approx C|e_n|^p$$

for some $p > 1$, the method converges faster than linearly. The value of $p$ is called the order of convergence.

  • Linear ($p = 1$): error reduces by a constant factor each step (e.g., bisection)
  • Superlinear ($1 < p < 2$): faster than linear, slower than quadratic (e.g., secant)
  • Quadratic ($p = 2$): correct digits roughly double each step (e.g., Newton)
